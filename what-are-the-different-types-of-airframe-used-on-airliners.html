What are the different types of airframe used on airliners?

When it comes to commercial airliners, there are many different types of airframes used for different tasks. Each type has its own strengths and weaknesses, which is why so many different designs are used. In this article we will take a look at some of the most common types of airframes used in commercial aviation. An airframe is the basic structure of an airplane that holds everything else together. It can be thought of as the skeleton of a plane, with all the internal parts and support systems connected to it. Different types of airframes are used depending on the type of operation and the type of passengers they’re carrying. There are four basic types of airframes: Wide-body, narrow-body, medium-body, and regional transport (or RTA) airframes. Each one has its own unique characteristics that make it suitable for different types of missions.

Wide-body Airframes

Wide-body airframes are the largest of the four main types of commercial airframes. They’re also sometimes called “super widebody” or “giant widebody” airframes. Wide-body airframes are designed to carry the most passengers and/or cargo, and are usually used on long-haul flights. Wide-body airframes can also be used for shorter flights, but only if there is an extra passenger or two who needs to be seated in the main cabin. Wide-body airframes usually have a length of about 45 meters (150 ft), a wingspan of about 90 meters (295 ft), and a height of about 17 meters (55 ft). Wide-body airframes are also known as “4-engine” or “5-engine” airframes because they usually have four engines.

Narrow-body Airframes

Narrow-body airframes are the smallest of the four main types of commercial airframes. They’re also sometimes called “super narrowbody” or “giant narrowbody” airframes. Narrow-body airframes are best for short-haul flights, or flights that stop at a few major hubs along the way. Narrow-body airframes usually have a length of about 30 meters (98 ft), a wingspan of about 45 meters (148 ft), and a height of about 10 meters (33 ft). Narrow-body airframes are also known as “2-engine” or “3-engine” airframes because they usually have two or three engines. Narrow-body airframes are also known as “single aisle” or “2-abreast” airframes because they have two aisles instead of three.

Medium-body Airframes

Medium-body airframes are a bit smaller than narrow-body airframes, but not as small as wide-body airframes. Medium-body airframes are best for regional flights, or for flights that stop at a few major hubs along the way but don’t go all the way to the destination city. Medium-body airframes usually have a length of about 30 meters (98 ft), a wingspan of about 45 meters (148 ft), and a height of about 10 meters (33 ft). Medium-body airframes are also known as “2-engine” or “3-engine” airframes because they usually have two or three engines. Medium-body airframes are also known as “2-abreast” airframes because they have two aisles instead of three.

Regional Transport Airframes

Regional transport airframes are used on smaller regional flights, such as flights within a country or between countries that are not connected by a large hub. Regional transport airframes usually have a length of about 20 meters (66 ft), a wingspan of about 30 meters (98 ft), and a height of about 5 meters (16 ft). Regional transport airframes are also known as “2-engine” or “3-engine” airframes because they usually have two or three engines. Regional transport airframes are also known as “1-abreast” airframes because they have one aisle instead of two.

Conclusion

The type of airframe used on an airplane depends on the type of flight and the number of passengers and/or cargo. Wide-body airframes are usually used for long-haul flights, while narrow-body airframes are used for shorter flights. The type of airframe used also depends on the type of engine used. Wide-body airframes usually have four engines, while narrow-body airframes usually have two or three engines. The type of airframe used also depends on the type of airport the airplane will be landing at. Wide-body airframes are usually used at large commercial airports, while narrow-body airframes are usually used at smaller regional airports.
