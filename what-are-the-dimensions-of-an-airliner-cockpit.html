What are the dimensions of an airliner cockpit?

The cockpit of an airliner is where the pilot and co-pilot sit and operate the airplane. It’s also where they can see everything happening in the cabin and outside the airplane. The cockpit of a large commercial airliner is an expansive space, with multiple control panels, computer screens, and other instruments. Although the layout of an airplane’s cockpit varies from one type of plane to another, most have the following main components: two seats for the pilot and co-pilot, a flight deck, a control console, and a navigation station. The following are details about the dimensions of an airliner cockpit:

Dimensions of an Airbus A380’s Cockpit

The A380 is the largest commercial airliner ever built. With a length of more than 200 feet and a wingspan of nearly the same length, it has a very spacious cockpit. The A380’s flight deck measures 80 by 30 feet. The A380’s cockpit is also home to the most advanced avionics ever installed in an airplane, with more than 100 electronic components and sensors.The A380’s cockpit is split into two distinct sections. The forward section of the flight deck houses the flight controls and has a single seat for the pilot. The aft section is primarily used for monitoring the airplane’s systems and has two seats for the co-pilot.The A380’s flight deck is equipped with state-of-the-art avionics, including a high-definition colour display, a synthetic vision system, a moving map display, a synthetic voice system, and a satellite navigation system.

Dimensions of an Airbus A380’s Cabin
